Welcome! You're about to have your own personal BookTok Command
Center — all your books, content, hashtags, and captions in one
place, working like a real app on your phone.

This takes about 10 minutes. No coding needed.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 1 — Download the file
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

You should have received one file: ShelfControl.html

Save it somewhere easy to find on your computer.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 2 — Test it on your computer first
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Double-click ShelfControl.html. It will open in your browser
(Chrome, Safari, Firefox — any of them work).

A welcome screen will appear asking for your BookTok name and
your hashtag. Fill those in and tap "Set Up My App."

That's it — your app is now personalized. Take a look around!


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 3 — Put it online so you can use it on your phone
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

GitHub Pages hosts your app for free. Here's how:

  1. Go to github.com and create a free account
     (use any email — you just need to be signed in)

  2. Click the "+" button in the top right corner
     → Select "New repository"

  3. Name it anything you want (example: my-booktok-app)
     → Set it to Public
     → Click "Create repository"

  4. Click "uploading an existing file"
     → Drag your ShelfControl.html file into the upload box
     → IMPORTANT: Before saving, rename it to:  index.html
       (click the file name in the upload box and change it)
     → Scroll down and click "Commit changes"

  5. Go to Settings (top menu of your repository)
     → Click "Pages" in the left sidebar
     → Under "Branch" select "main" → click Save

  6. Wait about 60 seconds, then refresh the page.
     You'll see a green box with your app's link — something
     like:  yourusername.github.io/my-booktok-app

     That link is your app. Bookmark it!


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 4 — Add it to your phone home screen
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  On iPhone / iPad:
  1. Open your app link in Safari (must be Safari, not Chrome)
  2. Tap the Share button (the box with an arrow pointing up)
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Give it a name and tap "Add"

  On Android:
  1. Open your app link in Chrome
  2. Tap the three dots menu (top right)
  3. Tap "Add to Home screen"
  4. Tap "Add"

  Your app now lives on your home screen like a real app.
  It works offline too — no wifi needed once it's installed.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  STEP 5 — Sync to a second device (iPad, phone, etc.)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Your app saves everything locally on your device. To move your
data to another device:

  1. Open your app and tap "Backup My Data"
     → This downloads a small file with all your books,
       content calendar, notes, and everything else

  2. Use AirDrop (iPhone/iPad) or email/messages to send
     that file to your other device

  3. On the other device, open your app link and tap
     "Restore Backup" → select the file

  Done! Both devices now have the same data.


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: Will I lose my data if I clear my browser?
A: Yes — your data is saved on your device, not the cloud.
   Use "Backup My Data" regularly, just like saving a document.
   Once a week is a good habit.

Q: Can I change my name or hashtag later?
A: Yes! Tap the small ⚙️ gear icon in the bottom right corner
   of the app anytime.

Q: Does this cost anything to keep running?
A: No. GitHub Pages is free forever for public files like this.

Q: Can I use this without putting it online?
A: Yes! Just open the file directly in your browser on your
   computer. The only downside is you can't use it on your
   phone that way.

Q: Something looks wrong / a feature isn't working.
A: Try clearing your browser cache and reloading.
   If that doesn't fix it, reach out to the creator.
